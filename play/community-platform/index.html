Community Platform Diagnostics

A platform with many passive users may still feel socially dead, while a smaller but highly interactive community becomes self-sustaining. I built this in NetLogo to find the threshold.

Tool
NetLogo
Year
2026
Type
Play
Topic
Complex Systems

Users move through five states: newcomer, passive, active, host, and churned. Most newcomers slide into passivity. A few convert to active — and a small fraction of those start hosting events. The question is what happens at each transition. Adjust referral rate, event quality, or host recovery time and the equilibrium shifts — sometimes catastrophically.

Host burnout is the real bottleneck. Lose your hosts and the community goes passive in about 40 ticks.

What surprised me: growth doesn't stabilize communities, it stresses them. A referral spike floods the system with newcomers, but if host capacity can't absorb them, satisfaction drops and churn follows. The platform design decisions that matter most aren't acquisition levers — they're the ones that protect the small group doing the actual work.
