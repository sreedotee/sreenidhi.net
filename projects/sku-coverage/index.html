SKU Coverage B2B Design

SKU Coverage is a B2B SaaS dashboard for managing AI-generated product imagery at scale. It's built for fashion and e-commerce teams that produce visual assets across many SKUs and channels, and need a single place to see what's ready, what's in flight, and what's costing what. The product sits at the intersection of digital asset management and AI workflow ops. The design is currently in active development.

Teams using a tool like this aren't generating one image at a time. They're shipping hundreds of looks per launch, across product shots, model imagery, lifestyle scenes, and channel-specific crops. Without a coordination layer, this work scatters across Slack threads, shared drives, and individual generation tools. The dashboard consolidates that scatter into one source of truth: per-SKU asset coverage across channels, status, and ownership.

Work in progress

The product is structured around a few core objects. SKUs are the products, Assets are the generated images grouped by channel and use case, Workflows are the pipelines that produce them, Models are the trained AI models the workflows draw from, and Credits are the generation budget. Each object has its own surface for filtering and drilling in, and they cross-reference, so an operator can move from a missing asset back to the workflow that should have produced it without losing context.

Work in progress

B2B tools at this density fail when they try to surface everything at once. I built the IA around the operator's most common starting question, which is some version of what's blocking the next launch?. The default views answer that first, and let the operator drill from there into specific SKUs, models, or workflows. The denser admin surfaces (model management, credit reconciliation, settings) live one level deeper so they're available without crowding the daily flow.
