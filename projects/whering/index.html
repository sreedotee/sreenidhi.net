Whering Redesign

Whering is a digital wardrobe app for cataloguing your clothes, building outfits, planning what to wear, and browsing friends' wardrobes. Its positioning is buy less, and better. I led an end-to-end redesign focused on the information architecture, which was fragmented across overlapping surfaces.

The core issue was structural, not visual. Multiple surfaces shared the same job at the same level of the hierarchy. Canvas and Dress Me were both outfit builders. Plan and Planner had overlapping models. Wardrobe, Wishlist, Moodboard, and Lookbook were four containers serving one underlying behavior: grouping items.

Casual and power-user actions also sat side by side in the primary nav, flattening priority. Two key user journeys, creating an outfit and planning one for a specific day, failed under this structure.

I consolidated the surface inventory against a cleaner object model. Canvas and Dress Me merged into Style, a single outfit-building surface. Wardrobe, Wishlist, Moodboard, and Lookbook merged into Collections, one flexible grouping primitive. Outfits from all sources (created, discovered, photographed) now resolve to a single Outfit object.

Work in progress

The resulting IA is a five-tab structure (Discover, Search, Style, Planner, Profile) that keeps casual loops at the top level and demotes power-user surfaces one tier down, so the product scales with user depth instead of overwhelming on entry.
